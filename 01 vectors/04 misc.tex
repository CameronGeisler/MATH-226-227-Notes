\section*{Intersection Between Line and Plane}

\begin{example}
Find the intersection between the plane $x+y+z=-12$ and the line given by,
\begin{align*}
    \begin{cases}
        x = 1 + 2t \\
        y = -2 + 6t \\
        z = 2 + 5t
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
We want to find $t$ such that the point given by line lies on the plane. In other words, find $t$ such that $x + y + z = -12$ for the line's coordinates. To do this, substitute the coordinates, and solve for $t$,
\begin{align*}
    (1+2t) + (-2+6t) + (2+5t) & = -12 \\
    1 + 13t & = -12 \\
    13t & = -13 \\
    t & = -1
\end{align*}
Then, the point is,
\begin{align*}
    x & = 1 + 2(-1) = -1 \\
    y & = -2 + 6(-1) = -8 \\
    z & = 2 + 5(-1) = -3
\end{align*}
Thus, they intersect at the point $(-1,-8,-3)$.
\end{example}
